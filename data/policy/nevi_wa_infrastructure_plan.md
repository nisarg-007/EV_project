# NEVI Formula Program - Washington State Deployment Plan

## Funding Allocation
Washington is slated to receive approximately **$71 Million** over five years (2022–2027) from the National Electric Vehicle Infrastructure (NEVI) Formula Program.

## Primary Objective
The goal is to build a "backbone" of DC Fast Charging (DCFC) stations along designated Alternative Fuel Corridors (AFCs).

## Station Requirements
To qualify for NEVI funding, stations must:
- **Be located within 1 mile** of a designated highway/interstate.
- **Have at least 4 ports** capable of simultaneous 150kW charging (600kW total site power).
- **Be open 24/7** to the public.
- **Be no more than 50 miles apart** along the AFC.

## Designated Corridors in Washington
- I-5 (North/South)
- I-90 (East/West)
- I-82 / US-97
- US-395

## Current Status
WSDOT is currently in the process of awarding grants to private partners to build and operate these stations. The focus is on filling gaps in rural and underserved areas to ensure seamless long-distance travel.
