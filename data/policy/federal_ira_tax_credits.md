# Federal Clean Vehicle Tax Credits (Inflation Reduction Act)

## Expiration Warning
**All consumer federal EV tax credits (new and used) effectively end for vehicles acquired after September 30, 2025.**

## 1. New Clean Vehicle Credit (Section 30D)
**Maximum Credit:** $7,500
- **Battery Component Requirement:** $3,750 (Must be manufactured/assembled in North America).
- **Critical Minerals Requirement:** $3,750 (Must be extracted/processed in the US or a free-trade partner).

### Eligibility Requirements:
- **Vehicle MSRP Caps:** 
    - Vans, SUVs, Pickups: $80,000
    - All other vehicles (Sedans): $55,000
- **Final Assembly:** Must occur in North America.
- **Income Limits (Adjusted Gross Income):**
    - Married Filing Jointly: $300,000
    - Head of Household: $225,000
    - All other filers: $150,000

## 2. Used Clean Vehicle Credit (Section 25E)
**Maximum Credit:** 30% of sale price, capped at $4,000.

### Eligibility Requirements:
- **Sale Price:** Must be $25,000 or less.
- **Model Year:** Must be at least 2 model years older than the current calendar year.
- **Purchase:** Must be from a licensed dealer.
- **Income Limits:**
    - Married Filing Jointly: $150,000
    - Head of Household: $112,500
    - All other filers: $75,000

## 3. Point-of-Sale Transfer (New for 2024)
Starting January 1, 2024, buyers can transfer the tax credit to the dealer at the time of purchase. This effectively turns the tax credit into an **immediate discount** on the price of the car, rather than requiring the buyer to wait for their tax refund.

*Source: Internal Revenue Service (IRS), US Department of Energy (DOE).*
