# Washington State EV Registration Fees & Incentives (2024-2026)

## Overview
Washington State has implemented specific fee structures for Electric Vehicles (EVs) and Plug-in Hybrids (PHEVs) to support transportation infrastructure and electrification efforts.

## Registration Fees (Senate Bill 5801)
Starting October 1, 2025, the following annual registration fees apply to all-electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids with at least 30 miles of electric range:

| Fee Type | Amount | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Annual Registration Renewal** | $150 | Base fee for EVs/PHEVs |
| **Transportation Electrification Fee** | $75 | Dedicated to charging infrastructure |
| **Annual Indexing** | +2% | Starts July 1, 2026 (Inflation adjustment) |

*Note: Hybrid vehicles with less than 30 miles of range typically incur a $75–$100 total fee depending on specific legislative updates.*

## Sales and Use Tax Exemptions
- **New EVs:** Partial sales tax exemption for the first $15,000–$20,000 of the purchase price, provided the MSRP is below $45,000 (cars) or $59,000 (SUVs/Trucks).
- **Used EVs:** Exemption on the first $15,000–$16,000 of the purchase price for vehicles under $30,000.

## Washington EV Instant Rebate Program
- **Program Status:** Funding for the 2024/2025 cycle has been fully claimed as of mid-2026.
- **Eligibility:** Low-to-moderate-income residents (at or below 300% of federal poverty level).
- **Amount:** Up to $9,000 for new leases or $5,000 for new purchases.

## Local Utility Rebates
Various local utilities offer residential Level 2 charger rebates:
- **Tacoma Public Utilities:** Up to $400-600 rebate.
- **Snohomish PUD:** Rebates for smart chargers.
- **Seattle City Light:** Technical assistance and infrastructure grants.

*Source: Washington State Department of Licensing (DOL), Washington Department of Revenue (DOR).*
