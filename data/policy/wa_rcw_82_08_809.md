# RCW 82.08.809: Exemptions—Vehicles using clean alternative fuels and electric vehicles, exceptions—Quarterly transfers.

## Policy Overview
This statute established sales and use tax exemptions for vehicles using clean alternative fuels and electric vehicles in Washington State.

## Key Provisions
- **Definition of Clean Alternative Fuel:** Includes natural gas, propane, hydrogen, or electricity when used in a motor vehicle meeting California emission standards (Title 13).
- **Exemption Limits (Post-July 1, 2016):**
  - Applicable for up to $32,000 of a vehicle's selling price or total lease payments.
  - Ineligible if the lowest manufacturer's suggested retail price (MSRP) for the base model exceeds $42,500.
- **Previous Limits (July 2015 - June 2016):** Vehicles with a selling price or fair market value exceeding $35,000 were ineligible.
- **Expiration:** The exemption was designed to expire after the cumulative number of qualifying vehicles titled in the state reached 7,500, or by June 30, 2019, whichever came first.
- **Legislative Intent:** To induce designated behavior (purchasing clean vehicles) and reduce the price for customers to increase the use of alternative fuels.
