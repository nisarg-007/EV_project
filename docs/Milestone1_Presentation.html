The Problem We're Solving

⚠️  THE DATA DISCONNECT

Billions of dollars are being deployed blindly.

Quantitative data (Where are the cars?) and qualitative policy (What grants exist?) live in complete isolation.

Stakeholders spend weeks cross-referencing CSVs with 300-page government PDFs for a single decision.

CHALLENGE 1

Misallocated Capital

Charging stations placed by "gut feeling" or outdated traffic models become stranded assets — expensive hardware with zero users.

🎯 The Opportunity

Use hyper-local registration data at the zip-code level to predict exactly where adoption hotspots are naturally forming — before competitors arrive.

🚧 The Barrier

Raw government datasets are noisy, unstructured, and lack any actionable Business Intelligence layer. Nobody can extract insight without serious engineering effort.

CHALLENGE 2

Hidden Grid Strain

When 50 households in a single neighborhood each buy an EV, the local utility transformer can physically fail.

🎯 The Opportunity

Foresee geographic adoption trends and proactively upgrade infrastructure — before hardware fails.

🚧 The Barrier

Utilities only discover demand spikes when power draw surges and equipment overloads.

CHALLENGE 3

Policy Navigation Nightmare

The regulatory landscape is dense, fragmented, and changes every legislative session.

"Is a 2023 Tesla Model Y Long Range eligible for the Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle credit in King County if used for a commercial fleet?"

The Barrier: Finding this answer requires a policy expert to manually read through hundreds of pages of dense legislation — leaving millions in unclaimed incentives on the table.
